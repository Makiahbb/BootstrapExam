Nintendo 64

The Nintendo 64 was the first Nintendo console to feature 3D graphics. This is also the first system to have a controller with an analog stick. This Nintendo device has nearly 400 games. The newly renamed Nintendo console was fully unveiled to the public in playable form on November 24, 1995, at Nintendo's 7th Annual Shoshinkai trade show. The console was originally slated for release by Christmas of 1995. Though the release date was repeatedly delayed. The first delay was announced in April 1995, when Nintendo delayed the release to May 1996. The delay was due to the cartridge in this device preventing it from being able to display certain games. The second release date was later delayed again, to June 23, 1996. The company said the reason for this latest delay, and in particular, the cancellation of plans to release the console in all markets worldwide simultaneously, was that the company's marketing studies now indicated that they would not be able to manufacture enough units to meet demand by April 1996. But it was later introduced on June 23, 1996 then a while later released in Japan on September 29, 1996. Time named it Machine of the Year in 1996,[9] and in 2011, IGN named it the ninth-greatest video game console of all time.
